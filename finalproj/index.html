Problem Statement

Everyday, people are getting into traffic accidents around the country. However, where, when, and in what situations do these accidents occur most frequently?

In our project, we explore the the most common accident patterns from the past 4 years in order to potentially help prevent future accidents. To examine these patterns, we will use interactive visualization techniques to most effectively communicate the high frequency situations found in the data.

Motivation

Raising Awareness

It is important for people to be aware of the specific circumstances that could give rise to more frequent car accidents (such as specific types of roads, locations, and weather conditions) in order to prevent future accidents.

Emphasizing Relevance

Many of the most frequent locations for car accidents happen in our own backyard, and we might not even know it! We, as Californians, should realize the relevance and urgency of this issue to our own lives.

Looking Ahead

Analyzing the patterns of traffic accidents could give further insights and arguments for self-driving cars and technology.

Dataset

4 years, 49 US states, 3.0 billion accidents.

Counts by Location Types

The frequency of traffic accidents by location type is the highest at junctions, and the second highest at traffic signals.

Conclusion

Common Patterns

Through each of the visualizations, we see that there are common patterns that follow these traffic accidents, either as a result of reality or the constraints of the given dataset. However, we can see that junctions and highway entrances are highly accident-prone areas of the road. Also, traffic accidents tend to occur at a higher frequency during the dark winter months.

Prevention

With the knowledge of common patterns for traffic accidents that we know now, we can take a second to think and take more precaution the next time we enter a highway or take a drive when it is dark outside. Taking this extra step of caution will likelu help the prevention of future accidents and protect the safety of ourselves and that of others with whom we share the road.

Future Work

In this project, we both learned the many factors that can ultimately influence the likelihood of a car accident and some good and not so good ways of conveying these factors.

To extend this project further, our next steps would likely be incorporating even more interaction and transparency in the above visualizations by incorporating more information that we have from the dataset. We would also likely explore other factors that influence traffic accidents, such as weather conditions and time of day, and extend our findings to other parts of the country and world.
